My name is Brian Wyant.

And I'm here to present something approximating the most minimal site ever. Like zenhabits, except devoid of any actually useful content.

Really, there's a few things I like people to know about me, to get a better idea of what kind of person I am.

First, that I'm a software developer. For the past few years, I've worked for Oracle Primavera. Before that, I went to school at RIT for Software Engineering. I heartily approve of folks sharing their dotfiles on github, because I like pilfering new ideas. Presumably, I've also done one or two other things on github as well. I guess I also have a Linkedin account you could use for looking at some of my other history and projects.

I'm also an avid student of different topics in psychology - my minor at RIT was in psychology. Among other things, I'm amazed that there's so many biases in human behaviors and belief. Indeed, I wonder how I could - if I could - promote non-biased behavior in my own life. But of course, that's a massively long-term kind of project.

Lately, I have no less interest in philosphy than I do psychology. I've been a student of Zen for quite some time. And a student of ancient Stoicism - of thinkers in a rather different world than ours, but with their ideas being no less relevant for it. Having all this information online - having entire programs out there for free - in my opinion, demonstrates the promise of the internet.

I've lived, since my graduation, in Philadelphia. Moving here, I wanted to live somewhere I could get away without a car - indeed, I SEPTA Philly. My time here has been good to me, but lately I've been forming new plans. It's quite simple, really: when I visit a place and walk away impressed, I have reason to think about the structure of my life.

All this, and I haven't even mentioned... I really enjoy rock climbing and ultimate frisbee. Great sports.

So, that's what I've got to say. Thanks for reading!
